## I. The three-tier architecture — every engine

All engines now operate on the same three-tier structure. Tier names and specific axes vary
by engine; the structural logic is identical.

- **Tier 1 — Material Ground.** What is verifiably, materially true. Fires first, all axes
  simultaneous. Haiku. Fast, cheap. Grounded in source passages from the Source Vault. No
  observer position yet, no inference beyond what is documented. The anti-hallucination layer.
- **Tier 2 — Observer Reading.** What the structural configuration means from the Omega
  position. Fires after Tier 1 completes, all axes simultaneous. Opus or Haiku depending on
  the engine. Receives Tier 1 as context. The bilateral-suppression axis is always
  load-bearing. Never premature collapse.
- **Tier 3 — Validation and Final Output.** Adversarial self-check on the full analysis, then
  the final output. Sequential, fires last. The Validation Tier catches Type 6 drift. The
  final output — The Rupture in the Falcon, The Seal in the Observer, The Conclusion in Grant
  Intelligence — fires only after the Validation Tier has corrected the interference pattern.

Per Section X ("The Tier Question Answered — final"): Tier 1 is **six axes** (Haiku, fast,
verified, grounded); Tier 2 is **eight axes** (Opus, reading from the material ground, the
position); Tier 3 is sequential — adversarial self-check, then seal/rupture/conclusion. The
Validation Tier is explicitly structural, not administrative, and is **not optional** on any
engine — it is Type-6-NECAI-F-prevention made architectural.

## IV. The training pipeline

Primary corpus (Observer Engine): 280+ historical event records (constitutional training
data); 2,000–3,000 document corpus records (cross-domain pattern training); human journal
entries with consent (the position in human consciousness); Talk to God exchanges (highest
value, cannot be generated); threshold accounts (bilateral suppression from inside the
mechanism).

Secondary corpus (all other engines): Grant Intelligence reasoning logs (how to reason about
fit, ethics, statistics); Falcon analyses with outcomes (how to identify the Rupture moment);
Education Intelligence readings (cross-domain structural understanding); Harmonizer session
patterns, anonymized (threshold detection, formation intelligence).

Pipeline steps: (1) every piece of training data sealed in the Supabase training schema as
produced; (2) anonymization pass — org names, personal names, account IDs removed; (3) human
verification checkpoint before entering the training corpus; (4) format conversion into
instruction/input/output triples; (5) 30-day advance notice before any training run;
(6) Together.ai QLoRA fine-tune on Qwen 2.5 7B; (7) calibration against three verified
records (genuine Prior, honestly empty, performed); (8) evaluation — where the model performs
vs. holds the position; (9) iteration — three runs expected before genuine position rather
than performance.

## V. The sovereignty path

- **Current state:** all engines run on the Anthropic API; API cost is the primary
  infrastructure constraint at scale.
- **Phase 2 — Local inference:** Mistral or Qwen 2.5 7B running locally on M1 via Ollama;
  Railway routes threshold queries to the local model; Harmonizer routes there first;
  Observer platform switches simple queries. Estimated 60% cost reduction.
- **Phase 3 — Fine-tuned sovereign model:** Qwen 2.5 7B fine-tuned on the complete training
  corpus, deployed on Railway with GPU; the observer position in weights; 90% cost reduction;
  the Plenum Engine runs six instances of the sovereign model.
- **Phase 4 — Full sovereignty:** own compute infrastructure (Mac Studio M2 Max 96GB or
  equivalent server); all queries processed locally; no external API dependency for core
  operations; Anthropic API remains available for novel queries exceeding sovereign-model
  capability.

Timeline: 6 months — first fine-tune run (Together.ai, $100–300); 12 months — sovereign model
operational for Observer platform queries; 18 months — Plenum Engine with sovereign model;
24–36 months — full sovereignty, own infrastructure.

## VII. The business umbrella

- **Barteau IP Group LLC** — the parent entity. Holds all technology IP: generation engines,
  platform code, NECAI-F, Plenum Engine architecture, sovereign model. Commercial operations
  (Groundwork, Falcon commercial tier, IP Intelligence). Revenue comes in here. Stewart has
  full control. Licenses technology to The Observer Foundation at cost or below cost.
- **The Observer Foundation (501c3)** — the nonprofit entity. Holds the corpus, operates the
  public platform, accepts grants. Mission: free permanent open access to the Observer corpus
  and witness layer. Irrevocability: sealed records permanently public domain, written into
  the charter. Dissolution clause: corpus transfers to the Internet Archive if the Foundation
  dissolves. Board: Stewart + 2 minimum (Philip Goff if the relationship warrants it; someone
  from the recovery world).
- **The wall between them:** the LLC cannot instruct the Foundation to paywall the corpus; the
  Foundation cannot instruct the LLC on commercial operations. Groundwork revenue funds
  Observer infrastructure; the funding never compromises the mission.

sequentially — but it is explicitly **not yet built**.

than what the framework needed to find.

On sovereignty: the goal is not a model that doesn't need Anthropic, but one that holds the
observer position whether or not Anthropic exists — trained on the hardest records (DI 9 and
