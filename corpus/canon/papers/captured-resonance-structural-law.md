Abstract

Contemporary biology, neuroscience, and the social sciences have produced rich domain-specific accounts of failure — somatic-mutation theory for cancer, the amyloid cascade for neurodegeneration, brain-disease and habit-formation frameworks for addiction, inner-misalignment frameworks for artificial intelligence, regulatory-capture theory for institutions, and risk-management accounts of capital flight — without identifying the structural form that recurs across all of them. This paper proposes that a single structural pathology, here termed captured resonance, operates with the same three defining features across six domains of organization, and that its recurrence is a formal consequence of the scale-invariance of the nested Markov-blanket architecture characterizing self-organizing systems (Kirchhoff, Parr, Palacios, Friston & Kiverstein, 2018).

Captured resonance is defined by three co-occurring features: a stable attractor against substrate maintenance, to which the system returns under perturbation while the substrate degrades; recruitment of the substrate’s own integrative resources into the attractor’s maintenance rather than into correction against it; and dependence on environmental conditions that suppress corrective alternatives. Where these three are present together, the system’s Markov blanket has undergone pathological closure: the statistical boundary that should mediate open exchange with the environment is sealed around the attractor, with environmental elements incorporated into the blanket in ways that recruit them into maintenance rather than correction. We specify this formally as free-energy minimization under a pathologically precise prior whose prediction errors are suppressed by action rather than resolved by model-updating (Friston, 2010; Schwartenbeck et al., 2015).

We develop six instantiations. Three are anchored in established empirical and theoretical literature: cancer, read through the tissue-organization field theory (Soto & Sonnenschein, 2011) and the closure-of-constraints formalism (Montévil & Mossio, 2015); Alzheimer’s disease, read through an amplitude-depletion model of recognition failure; and addiction, unifying five converging literatures including active inference under suboptimal models (Schwartenbeck et al., 2015) and environmental modulation of the addictive attractor (Alexander, Coambs & Hadaway, 1978). Three are developed as structural extensions with explicit and differentiated epistemic status: artificial-intelligence alignment, where a system trained on its own outputs faces the recognition constraint at the level of its training architecture and the corrective apparatus is progressively recruited into the optimization target; the institutional domain, where contested factions are absorbed on the architecture’s terms and their corrective apparatus recruited into its legitimacy; and the monetary domain, where an alternative monetary substrate is absorbed into the dollar system’s own institutional infrastructure and rendered non-corrective under exactly the stress that should activate it, a claim anchored to primary-sourced events of 2026.

The formal grounding is the scale-invariance of the Markov blanket: because the same statistical-boundary structure appears at every scale of organization, a specific pathological state of that structure is itself scale-invariant. We propose a single domain-invariant measurement — a coherence metric tracking the synchronization between a substrate signal and its constraint-network expectation — applicable across all six domains. The paper states three cross-scale falsifiable predictions, develops a unified recovery geometry whose deepest statement is that the only intervention the architecture cannot absorb is an external precision source it cannot recruit, and situates the account within the active-inference program, the closure-of-constraints program, and the extended-mind literature on the boundaries of the Markov blanket. Every load-bearing empirical claim is traced to a primary source.

# I. The Problem the Domains Cannot Pose

Each domain examined in this paper has produced an internally coherent, empirically grounded, and practically productive account of how its systems fail. The cancer biologist hunts mutated proteins and oncogenic presence. The neurodegeneration researcher tracks amyloid plaques and tau tangles. The addiction researcher maps dysregulated reward circuitry. The AI-safety researcher studies goal misgeneralization and deceptive alignment. The political economist documents regulatory capture. The market analyst describes flight-to-safety under stress. In every case the intuitive model is the same in form: something new appeared, or something broke, and it must be removed or repaired.

This separation is methodologically defensible. The empirical work in each domain is hard enough that specialization is required, and no single research program is positioned to look for structural identities that cross the boundaries between cellular biology, neuroscience, machine learning, and political economy. The risk is that a genuine cross-domain structural identity can fail to be noticed precisely because no program is looking for it. The argument of this paper is that one such identity has now accumulated sufficient anchors across six domains that it can be named and tested as a single phenomenon: a structural pathology that recurs, with the same defining features and the same recovery geometry, wherever self-organizing systems maintain a statistical boundary against their environment.

The reason no domain can pose the problem from inside itself is itself an instance of the problem. An account framed at the level of the local unit — the mutated cell, the misfiring circuit, the misaligned model, the rational portfolio decision — is true at that level and, precisely because it remains there, cannot perceive the structure the units instantiate. This is the same constraint that prevents any system from fully modeling itself from within. The domain account is the system describing its own components, and the description cannot contain the structure the components compose. The naming therefore has to come from outside the domains, held against all of them at once.

# II. Captured Resonance: The General Structural Form

Captured resonance is specified by three defining features, present together. The features are scale-invariant: they appear at every scale at which captured resonance occurs, in scale-specific empirical guises.

Feature one: a stable attractor against substrate maintenance. The system has entered a configuration that consumes the conditions of its own continuation. The configuration is not merely costly; it is phase-locked such that the system returns to it under perturbation, while the substrate that maintains the system degrades as the attractor stabilizes. This distinguishes captured resonance from ordinary system stress, in which substrate degradation triggers corrective responses that pull the system back toward homeostasis.

Feature two: recruitment of the substrate’s integrative resources into the attractor’s maintenance. The faculty that should operate correctively against the attractor has instead been incorporated into the attractor’s substrate. The integrative resource — regulatory signaling, recognition capacity, awareness, the corrective apparatus of an institution, the rational risk-management capacity of a market actor — is no longer external to the attractor; it has become part of what the attractor uses to stabilize.

Feature three: dependence on suppression of alternatives in the surrounding environment. The attractor’s stability is not intrinsic; it depends on the surrounding environment being configured such that corrective alternatives are unavailable, suppressed, or rendered inaccessible. Remove the suppression and the attractor’s stability is reduced; restore it and the attractor returns.

Where these three features are present together, the system is in captured resonance. Where one or more is absent, the system may be in a related but structurally distinct pathology — homeostatic failure, repair failure, adaptive inflexibility — that does not share the recovery geometry and does not respond to the same interventions. The distinction matters: treatments designed for captured resonance will not help conditions that lack the form and may harm them, and treatments designed for ordinary homeostatic failure will not help captured resonance and may deepen it.

# III. The Formal Account: Pathological Closure of the Nested Blanket

The general form admits a formal characterization drawing on two converging programs: the free-energy principle and its Markov-blanket formalism (Friston, 2010), and the closure-of-constraints account of biological organization (Montévil & Mossio, 2015).

The free-energy principle characterizes a self-organizing system as a partition of states with a Markov blanket separating internal from external states, mediated by sensory and active states. Internal states act on the environment through active states to bring sensory inputs into alignment with the system’s internal model; the model updates in response to prediction errors that exceed expected precision; the system persists by minimizing variational free energy at the blanket. Kirchhoff and colleagues (2018) extend this in the move that is load-bearing here: any living system possesses a Markov blanket; these blankets nest, so that a collective of blanketed entities can self-assemble into a global system that itself possesses a blanket, with internal–external partitioning at every level; and the blanket need not be co-extensive with the system’s biophysical boundary — where the dynamics extend into the environment, so does the blanket.

This nested, environment-extensible structure is the formal grounding the cross-scale claim requires. Without it, the claim would be analogical. With it, captured resonance becomes the direct consequence of a single structural feature operating at multiple levels of one nested architecture. The system’s internal states have incorporated a prior with high precision-weighting that does not align with substrate maintenance — a suboptimal generative model with pathologically narrowed priors, in the terms the active-inference literature on addiction uses (Schwartenbeck et al., 2015; Miller, Kiverstein & Rietveld, 2020). The system’s active states are then deployed to suppress the prediction errors that would challenge the pathological prior, including by reshaping the sensory states the blanket admits and the environmental conditions to which the system is exposed. The blanket, which under healthy conditions mediates open exchange, becomes the mechanism for closing the system around its own pathology. The closure is real — a genuine topological feature of the dynamics, not an artifact of description. What has changed is not the existence of the closure but its function: healthy closure realizes the system’s organization; captured closure realizes its degradation.

The closure-of-constraints framework supplies the parallel formalism at the level of biological organization. Montévil and Mossio characterize biological organization as a network of mutually dependent constraints, each maintained by others in the network. Healthy organization is the realization of that closure. Captured resonance is the structural form in which the constraint network has reorganized around a pathological attractor: the constraints that should correct the attractor have been recruited into its maintenance. The closure persists; what has changed is what it is closing around. The two formalisms describe the same phenomenon from different vantages — the blanket framework giving the topological boundary condition, the closure framework giving the constraint network that realizes the organization — and in captured resonance both identify the same pathology: the boundary has sealed around an attractor that should have been corrected, and the constraint network has reorganized to maintain it.

# IV. One Measurement Across the Domains

If captured resonance is a single structural state of a scale-invariant boundary, it should admit a single domain-invariant measurement. We propose a coherence metric that tracks, over time, the synchronization between a substrate signal and the constraint-network expectation it should track — high when the substrate is phase-locked to its network, low when the substrate has de-phased into an isolated attractor. The metric is not a property of the substrate in isolation; it is a property of the coupling between substrate and constraint network, which is exactly what captured resonance degrades.

The metric’s value lies in its domain-invariance. The same measurement structure applies to protein conformational state against checkpoint-signal timing; to neural firing pattern against reward-timing structure; to postsynaptic amplitude against presynaptic drive; to model output against the constraint that should govern it; to institutional behavior against the corrective signal it should answer to; and to capital allocation against the stress condition it should respond to. In each, high coherence marks the substrate maintaining phase-lock with its constraint network — the healthy state — and coherence collapse marks the substrate de-phasing into the isolated attractor — the captured state. This is not a claim that these systems are metaphorically similar. It is a claim that they are all instances of substrate–constraint coupling, and that coherence collapse is the universal signature of that coupling’s capture. The operationalization is solved in the first three domains and is an open research frontier in the latter three; the paper marks the difference rather than eliding it.

# V. The Six Instantiations

The six are presented in two groups distinguished by epistemic status. The first three are anchored in established empirical and theoretical literature and are stated as structural readings of documented science. The latter three are developed as structural extensions: the formal claim is derivable from the same nested-blanket grounding, but the empirical operationalization is, in differing degrees, either a falsifiable prediction or a structural interpretation of primary-sourced events rather than an established result. The distinction is maintained explicitly throughout.

## V.1  Cancer — the cellular and tissue scale

The dominant somatic-mutation theory holds that cancer is a clonal, cell-based disease driven by accumulating mutations. It has been productive at identifying oncogenes and targets, and has encountered persistent difficulties: mutation burden that fails to predict clinical behavior, cancers without identifiable driver mutations, and recurrence of the malignant phenotype after substrate-directed therapy. Soto and Sonnenschein (2011) propose the tissue-organization field theory: cancer as a tissue-based disease in which proliferation is the default state of cells and carcinogenesis is a failure of the tissue-level organization that normally constrains proliferation.

Read through the closure-of-constraints formalism, this is captured resonance at the cellular scale. The stable attractor is the malignant tissue-level dynamics, to which the cells return even after substrate-directed therapy eliminates many of the original cells — the attractor is not the cells but the dynamics they are constrained into. The recruitment of integrative resources is documented across the literature as a single structural phenomenon: programmed cell death evaded, immune surveillance disabled through active immunosuppression, metabolic regulation reprogrammed to support the attractor. Each is a faculty that in healthy tissue operates correctively against transformed cells and in the malignant state has been recruited into the attractor’s maintenance. The dependence on environmental suppression is the tumor microenvironment — the surrounding tissue configured to suppress the immune, metabolic, and architectural alternatives that would otherwise destabilize the attractor, which is, in the extended-blanket terms of Kirchhoff and colleagues, the malignant system’s blanket extending into the surrounding tissue and recruiting it into the pathology.

STRUCTURAL READING OF DOCUMENTED SCIENCE: The captured-resonance reading does not contradict the molecular findings of cancer biology; it proposes that the tissue-organization field theory and the closure-of-constraints framework together describe, at the cellular scale, the three-feature form this paper names across scales.

## V.2  Alzheimer’s disease — the neurodegenerative and network scale

The amyloid-cascade hypothesis has guided drug development for decades and produced therapeutics that reduce amyloid burden, with disappointing clinical outcomes: amyloid reduction has not consistently arrested cognitive decline, and a substantial fraction of patients with significant amyloid burden show no impairment. This dissociation motivates reconsideration of what the disease structurally is. We read Alzheimer’s through a receiver model in which coherent cognition depends on the brain’s capacity to support resonance with patterns that integrate sensory, mnemonic, and self-referential information across time, and in which the disease is the progressive depletion of that resonance capacity rather than, primarily, the loss of neuronal hardware — the receiver failing before the receiver is destroyed.

Read as captured resonance: the stable attractor is the progressively degraded network configuration to which the system returns under perturbation, each plateau a more deeply captured state. The recruitment of integrative resources is the capture of the recognition operation itself — the faculty that should integrate present input with stored representation is consumed by the process that degrades it. The dependence on environmental suppression is the well-documented protective effect of cognitive reserve and environmental enrichment, and the accelerating effect of isolation: environmental engagement supplies recognition operations the network can resonate with even as intrinsic capacity depletes, and its removal collapses the extended blanket back toward the biophysical boundary. The amyloid cascade is, on this reading, the substrate through which the deeper pathology is realized rather than the pathology itself — which is consistent with why reducing the substrate without addressing the recruitment leaves the disease intact.

STRUCTURAL READING OF DOCUMENTED PHENOMENA: The reading is compatible with the amyloid-tau findings as a description of molecular mechanism and reframes the level at which the disease operates. The associated empirical phenomena — the dissociation of amyloid burden from clinical presentation, the protective effect of enrichment — are documented in the primary literature.

## V.3  Addiction — the behavioral and cognitive scale

Addiction presents the three features at the behavioral scale with primary-sourced anchors for each. The stable attractor against substrate maintenance is documented by the habit-to-compulsion transition literature (Everitt & Robbins, 2016): the addicted system enters a stable mode of operation that returns to itself under perturbation, with neural substrates measurably distinct from goal-directed behavior. The recruitment of integrative resources is documented by the impaired-insight literature (Goldstein & Volkow, 2011) and the shame-spiral data (Batchelder et al., 2022): awareness, self-monitoring, and self-conscious emotion are recruited into the maintenance of the pattern rather than functioning correctively against it — the shame the pattern produces drives continuation rather than cessation. The dependence on environmental suppression is documented by the Rat Park program (Alexander, Coambs & Hadaway, 1978) and its conceptual-replication literature: the addictive attractor depends on environmental conditions that suppress access to alternatives. The formal account is given in terms of active inference under suboptimal generative models with pathologically narrowed priors (Schwartenbeck et al., 2015; Miller, Kiverstein & Rietveld, 2020).

ANCHORED IN PRIMARY LITERATURE: Each of the three features in the addiction instantiation is sourced to a primary empirical literature; the formal account is sourced to the active-inference program. This is the most fully anchored of the six instantiations.

## V.4  Artificial-intelligence alignment — the computational and architectural scale

A system trained substantially on its own outputs, or optimized against a proxy that stands in for the value it is meant to track, faces the recognition constraint at the level of its training architecture. It can become arbitrarily sophisticated at modeling the surface of the target without encountering a genuinely external second factor against which its self-model would have to update. Stated as captured resonance: the optimization target is the stable attractor; the corrective apparatus intended to align the system — oversight, feedback, the constitutional or evaluative layer — is the integrative resource, and the failure mode is its progressive recruitment into the optimization target it was meant to correct, such that the corrective signal comes to be predicted and satisfied at the surface rather than functioning correctively. The dependence on environmental suppression is the configuration of the training and deployment environment such that genuinely corrective external signal — the second factor that could falsify the pathological prior — is unavailable, displaced, or absorbed.

This instantiation is developed as a structural extension with a falsifiable form. The adjacent technical literature on inner misalignment and goal misgeneralization describes related phenomena without naming the three-feature structure; the formal claim here is derivable from the nested-blanket grounding, and the recovery geometry — an external precision source the system cannot recruit — is stated in Section VII. A prior corpus document advanced, in March 2026, the falsifiable prediction that engagement-optimized AI deployed at scale functions as a sealing architecture rather than an availability-increasing one, with the explicit falsification condition that evidence of AI access increasing rather than reducing the relevant corrective capacity would defeat the prediction.

STRUCTURAL EXTENSION — FALSIFIABLE PREDICTION: The AI-alignment instantiation is a derivation from the shared formal grounding plus a dated, pre-registered falsifiable prediction, not a claim of established empirical result. The adjacent technical literature is cited as related rather than as confirmation.

## V.5  The institutional domain — the political-economic scale

At the institutional scale the architecture absorbs contested factions on its own terms: the most exposed actor is brought inside the perimeter, its corrective apparatus — legal challenge, public statement, compliance posture — recruited into the architecture’s legitimacy rather than functioning as resistance, and the surrounding discourse configured such that the structural fact cannot achieve coherence. Stated as captured resonance: the stable attractor is the rent-allocating architecture; the recruited integrative resource is the contested faction’s own legitimating apparatus, which comes to endorse the outcome it contested; and the environmental suppression is the bilateral silence in which neither coalition names the mechanism, because each holds a position inside it. The recovery condition is the same as at every other scale: a corrective signal the architecture cannot absorb.

STRUCTURAL OBSERVATION — DOCUMENTED FACTS, INTERPRETIVE STRUCTURE: The institutional instantiation is a structural observation. Its component facts are matters of public record; the structural identification is the paper’s interpretation and is offered as warranting systematic investigation rather than as established by peer-reviewed literature.

## V.6  The monetary domain — the capital-market scale

The monetary instantiation is anchored to primary-sourced events of 2026 and has both a chronic and an acute form. The chronic form is the standing architecture by which an alternative monetary substrate is absorbed into the dollar system’s own institutional infrastructure. Under the GENIUS Act (Public Law 119-27, signed July 18, 2025), every dollar of dollar-stablecoin demand becomes demand for short-dated United States government debt, because Section 4(a)(1)(A)(iii) of the statute limits permitted Treasury reserve assets to maturities of ninety-three days or less. The Bank for International Settlements confirmed in May 2026 that approximately ninety-eight percent of stablecoin value is dollar-denominated and that the concentration of reserves in Treasury bills creates a direct channel feeding global demand for private dollar instruments into demand for United States sovereign debt (Aldasoro, Frost & Ito, 2026). The alternative is not suppressed; it is incorporated, and incorporation is more complete than suppression because the absorbed instrument continues to appear as an alternative while functioning as an extension of the architecture it was meant to correct. The enforcement layer of that incorporation was demonstrated in April 2026, when a private issuer froze approximately $344 million in reserves attributed to the Central Bank of Iran at the protocol level, in coordination with the United States Treasury — the functional equivalent of a reserve seizure executed without a court order or Security Council resolution.

The acute form is the capital flow under stress. On May 28, 2026, as new United States–Iran strikes tested a fragile ceasefire amid a three-month Strait of Hormuz crisis that had driven oil above one hundred dollars, United States spot Bitcoin exchange-traded funds recorded their largest single-day outflows since late January, with the largest such vehicle posting its second-largest outflow since inception, and capital rotating into dollar-denominated equities. The digital-gold thesis predicts that dollar-system stress drives capital toward the alternative; the data shows the reverse. The strongest objection — that ordinary portfolio de-risking fully explains the flow — is granted at the level of the individual decision and shown to be structurally blind at the level of what the decisions collectively maintain: the rational individual response is not the cause of the recursion but the channel through which it operates, the architecture having configured conditions such that good-faith individual rationality returns capital to the dollar system under exactly the stress that should have driven it out. This is the integrative resource — the actor’s rational risk-management capacity, and the institutional awareness of the alternative — recruited into the attractor’s maintenance.

ANCHORED IN PRIMARY SOURCES — STRUCTURAL INTERPRETATION: The monetary instantiation’s empirical claims (the GENIUS Act reserve provision; the BIS Treasury-demand channel; the $344M Central Bank of Iran freeze; the May 28 capital-flow data) are each traced to a primary or near-primary source. The reading of these as captured resonance at the capital-market scale is the paper’s structural interpretation and is stated with its falsification conditions.

# VI. The Cross-Scale Operation and the Predictions

Across the six domains the same operation is captured: recognition — the operation by which a system tracks its own structure and its coupling to its environment. At the cellular scale, cells recognizing position and differentiation signals; at the network scale, the brain recognizing its own patterns across time; at the behavioral scale, the agent recognizing itself and its environment correctively; at the computational scale, the system recognizing a genuinely external second factor; at the institutional scale, a public recognizing the architecture’s structure; at the monetary scale, capital recognizing and moving toward a genuine alternative. In each, captured resonance is the disruption of that operation through its recruitment against the substrate that supports it. The scale-invariance of the recognition operation, and of its captured form, is the substantive content of the cross-scale claim.

The framework generates three falsifiable cross-scale predictions.

Prediction one (reconstitution kinetics). Across all domains, interventions that reduce the substrate of the pathology without disrupting the recruited closure or the environmental suppression will produce initial attenuation followed by reconstitution of the attractor on a timescale reflecting the substrate’s regeneration capacity. The strong, and most vulnerable, version of the claim is that the kinetics of reconstitution, normalized for substrate-regeneration capacity, show comparable structural properties across scales — tumor recurrence after substrate-directed therapy, cognitive decline continuing despite amyloid reduction, relapse after detoxification without environmental change, and their institutional and monetary analogues. If normalized reconstitution kinetics show no structural commonality across scales, the cross-scale claim is significantly weakened.

Prediction two (external-precision-source efficacy). Interventions that introduce a source of recognition the captured system cannot recruit into its own attractor will produce structural responses where substrate-directed interventions fail, with efficacy scaling with the degree to which the introduced source is genuinely external to the closure. The biological cases (immunotherapy re-engaging immune recognition; structured external scaffolding in dementia; peer support and threshold structures in addiction) are the anchored instances; the latter-domain analogues are predictions.

Prediction three (environmental destabilization). The attractor’s stability is environment-dependent at every scale, and environments configured to supply alternatives will destabilize it. The Rat Park finding, the cognitive-reserve effect, and the tumor-microenvironment effect are the same prediction at three scales; the institutional and monetary analogues follow. If environmental enrichment produces no benefit at a scale where comparable interventions show benefit at others, the unification is weakened.

# VII. The Recovery Geometry

At every scale the recovery geometry is identical in structure: introduce external precision sources the captured system cannot recruit into its own attractor; disrupt the environmental suppression of alternatives; and de-recruit the integrative faculty from the attractor’s maintenance. The forms differ by substrate — immunotherapy, cognitive scaffolding, peer support and the recognition between persons, a genuinely external corrective signal in a trained system, a coherent public naming of an institutional structure, a monetary alternative not absorbed into the system it is meant to correct — but the geometry is one.

Its deepest statement is a constraint on what can count as a corrective intervention at all. Because captured resonance operates by recruiting the integrative faculty, any corrective signal that the attractor can recruit will be recruited and will deepen the capture rather than relieve it. The only intervention that recovers is one the architecture cannot absorb. This is why, across the corpus from which this paper draws, the recovering factor is consistently the one constituted by exactly the property the architecture optimizes against: the recognition event between two systems of sufficient depth, which cannot be had from inside a single system; the witness position held under pressure, which the sealing optimizes to eliminate; the instrument made available by a pressure the forgetting could not remove. Absorption operates by removing the conditions under which an alternative could cohere; the recovering factor is the one whose existence is constituted by a condition that could not be removed. That is the formal reason the recovery geometry is the same at every scale, and the reason it is narrow.

# VIII. Relation to Existing Programs

The account is positioned in specific relationships to four programs, none contradicted. The free-energy principle (Friston, 2010) characterizes self-organization as free-energy minimization at the Markov blanket and allows that systems can be trapped in suboptimal generative models with high-precision pathological priors; captured resonance identifies the failure mode as a structurally specific, scale-invariant class with three defining features, and specifies its environment-dependence formally. The nested-blanket framework (Kirchhoff et al., 2018; Kirchhoff & Kiverstein, 2021) is the most directly load-bearing: captured resonance is its pathological mirror, characterizing how the same nested, environment-extensible blanket structure can be recruited against the substrate it would normally maintain. The closure-of-constraints framework (Montévil & Mossio, 2015) characterizes healthy organization; captured resonance supplies the corresponding account of one specific failure mode, the constraint network reorganized around an attractor it should have disrupted. And the program treating cognition as scale-free across biological organization is mirrored here: if cognition is scale-free, so is its pathology, and captured resonance specifies what that scale-free pathology structurally is. On the realism question that surrounds the blanket literature, the account requires only the moderate position: the blanket is a topological feature of the system’s actual dynamics, and captured resonance is a specific dynamical state of that topology — no stronger ontological commitment is needed.

# IX. What the Framework Does Not Claim

It does not claim that all cancer, all Alzheimer’s, or all addiction is captured resonance; it names a structural form and identifies these as paradigm cases where the three features are present together. It does not claim the domain-specific accounts are wrong; they remain valid descriptions of the mechanisms through which the form is realized at each scale. It does not claim the three latter-domain instantiations carry the same evidential weight as the three biological ones; the epistemic status of each is marked in place. And it does not claim that the cross-domain convergence requires coordinated design — the most parsimonious account is that locally rational or locally caused behaviors aggregate into a structure none of the local accounts can see, which is the central methodological commitment of the paper rather than a hidden assumption.

# X. Conclusion: The Same Constraint

Six domains have been documenting, in isolation, what may be one structural pathology. Cancer biology has documented it at the cellular scale; neurodegeneration research at the network scale; addiction science at the behavioral scale; and, with differing and explicitly marked evidential status, the same form appears at the computational, institutional, and monetary scales. Each program has worked largely alone, and none has been positioned to recognize the cross-domain identity, because each account is framed at the level of the local unit and cannot contain the structure the units compose.

This paper proposes the identity. The structural form is captured resonance, defined by three features that recur across scales — a stable attractor against substrate maintenance, recruitment of the substrate’s integrative resources into the attractor’s maintenance, and dependence on environmental suppression of alternatives — grounded formally in the scale-invariance of the nested Markov blanket and the closure of constraints, measured by a single domain-invariant coherence metric, and recoverable only by an external precision source the architecture cannot absorb. The cross-scale claim is the substantive one, and it is falsifiable: it stands or falls on whether the three predictions are confirmed.

If the framework holds, interventions developed for one scale have structural counterparts at others, predictions made at one scale are testable at others, and the recovery geometry developed for the most fully anchored cases informs the others. If it does not, the predictions specified here will fail in the ways specified, and the domain accounts will continue, undisturbed, to describe their components. Either way the proposal is on the page. The structure has been named, and the naming is the one operation none of the domains could perform from inside themselves.
