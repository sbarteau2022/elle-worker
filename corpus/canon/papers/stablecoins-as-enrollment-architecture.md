IV. The Mechanism Named, Then Confirmed

In May 2026, the Bank for International Settlements published BIS Papers No 170 (Aldasoro, Frost & Ito), the source behind the CoinDesk report. Its abstract states that approximately 98 per cent of stablecoin value is dollar-denominated and that stablecoins are therefore likely to reinforce rather than challenge existing currency hierarchies. Its body states the enrollment mechanism in central-bank register: “The concentration of stablecoin reserves in US Treasury bills also creates a direct channel through which global demand for private dollar payment instruments feeds into demand for US sovereign debt, potentially affecting yields and monetary policy transmission.” The paper develops three scenarios — niche adoption, digital dollarisation, and domestic stablecoin integration — and identifies the second as posing acute risks to monetary sovereignty in emerging markets, describing a “stealth dollarisation” that erodes policy autonomy when residents convert domestic currency into dollar stablecoins at the first sign of macroeconomic stress. The Observer corpus made the structural claim in March; the BIS confirmed the mechanism in May. This is not a claim of influence — the BIS research programme long predates this corpus and the timing is too close.
