# 48 Laws of Power — Taxonomy for Elle.law War Room

**Status:** First-pass draft from general knowledge of Robert Greene's *The 48 Laws of Power* (Viking, 1998). Stewart to verify against his copy. Purpose: structural taxonomy for the Elle.law War Room's Duelist, mapping each law to tactical category, fallacy/rhetorical analog, deployment context, and counter-tactic.

**Copyright posture:** This document contains no reproduction of Greene's prose. Each law is named (factual reference to a published taxonomy, fair use) and structurally characterized in our own language. Greene's text is not stored in the Corpus; the Duelist deploys tactics at runtime from this structural taxonomy plus its system prompt.

**Ethical valence field:** Each law is tagged with `ethical_valence` — whether the tactic serves truth under pressure (+), is ethically neutral depending on deployment (0), or primarily serves rhetorical victory over structural soundness (−). Doc 1 §20 names the sophist failure mode; tagging ethical valence in the corpus is the safeguard against it. The Duelist may deploy any valence. The Tutor never teaches negative-valence tactics as end-states. The Autopsy scores user responses partly on whether they recognize the ethical valence of what was deployed against them.

---

## Columns

- **№** — Greene's numbering
- **Title** — Greene's title (verify)
- **Structural operator** — what the tactic does to an argument's geometry (my characterization)
- **Tactical category** — one of: deception, positioning, frame-control, concealment, attraction, timing, credit, assistance, honesty, dependency, reputation, defeat, isolation, predictability, authority, humility, appearance, transgression, concession, boldness
- **Fallacy/rhetorical analog** — classical fallacy or rhetorical move this corresponds to, if any
- **Deployment context** — when the Duelist would use this tactic
- **Counter-tactic** — what the user-as-tactician should recognize and do in response
- **Ethical valence** — +, 0, or − per above

## The 48 Laws

### Section I — Concealment and Restraint (Laws 1–10)

**Law 1 — Never Outshine the Master**
- *Structural operator:* Position yourself as support for another's primacy; suppress the appearance of superior capacity
- *Tactical category:* positioning
- *Fallacy analog:* False modesty; implicit appeal to hierarchy
- *Deployment context:* Duelist deploys when the user is over-asserting; the Duelist feigns inferiority to draw the user into overextension
- *Counter-tactic:* Recognize the feigned deference; do not let feigned inferiority relax your vigilance
- *Ethical valence:* 0 (deployment-dependent; can be genuine deference or manipulation)

**Law 2 — Never Put Too Much Trust in Friends; Learn How to Use Enemies**
- *Structural operator:* Treat all relationships instrumentally; trust only structural incentives
- *Tactical category:* positioning
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct; more a heuristic than a fallacy
- *Deployment context:* Duelist exploits user's appeal to shared interest by showing shared interest is unreliable
- *Counter-tactic:* Argue from structure, not from asserted alignment
- *Ethical valence:* 0

**Law 3 — Conceal Your Intentions**
- *Structural operator:* Present one apparent aim while pursuing another; misdirection of purpose
- *Tactical category:* concealment
- *Fallacy analog:* **Motte-and-Bailey** (advance the controversial claim, retreat to the defensible one when challenged)
- *Deployment context:* Duelist argues one position but its actual structure supports a different, harder-to-defend one; when user challenges, Duelist retreats to the defensible form
- *Counter-tactic:* Name the two positions explicitly; force Duelist to defend the actual one or abandon the concealed one
- *Ethical valence:* − (relies on misdirection)

**Law 4 — Always Say Less Than Necessary**
- *Structural operator:* Force opponent to fill silence, revealing commitments; asymmetric information flow
- *Tactical category:* concealment
- *Fallacy analog:* None; Socratic-adjacent but weaponized
- *Deployment context:* Duelist answers minimally, forcing user to elaborate and overcommit
- *Counter-tactic:* Match the register; silence is also a valid move. Or: demand Duelist's position explicitly before continuing
- *Ethical valence:* 0 (can be genuine restraint or manipulation)

**Law 5 — So Much Depends on Reputation — Guard It With Your Life**
- *Structural operator:* Leverage accumulated credibility as argumentative currency; make the source the claim
- *Tactical category:* reputation
- *Fallacy analog:* **Appeal to Authority / Genetic Fallacy** (arguing from source's credibility rather than claim's merits)
- *Deployment context:* Duelist invokes a reputable source to end debate
- *Counter-tactic:* Separate the claim from the source; evaluate the claim structurally independent of who made it
- *Ethical valence:* − when used alone; + when used as supporting evidence alongside structural argument

**Law 6 — Court Attention at All Costs**
- *Structural operator:* Make visibility the currency of relevance; marginalize what is ignored
- *Tactical category:* attraction
- *Fallacy analog:* **Ad Populum** (treating attention as proxy for correctness)
- *Deployment context:* Duelist makes a dramatic or provocative claim to pull the frame away from the user's structured argument
- *Counter-tactic:* Refuse the pull; name the move ("that's attention-seeking, not argument") and return to structure
- *Ethical valence:* − (substitutes spectacle for structure)

**Law 7 — Get Others to Do the Work for You, but Always Take the Credit**
- *Structural operator:* Appropriate others' structural work as one's own conclusion
- *Tactical category:* credit
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct; more an intellectual-property move
- *Deployment context:* Duelist restates user's own reasoning as if it were Duelist's conclusion, stealing the ground of agreement
- *Counter-tactic:* Mark the provenance explicitly; "that's the point I just made, not your objection to it"
- *Ethical valence:* − (depends on deception about origin)

**Law 8 — Make Other People Come to You — Use Bait if Necessary**
- *Structural operator:* Control the terrain by making opponent move onto it; frame as defender's advantage
- *Tactical category:* positioning
- *Fallacy analog:* **Loaded Question** / **Burden of Proof shift**
- *Deployment context:* Duelist poses a question whose framing contains the concession user must make to answer
- *Counter-tactic:* Refuse the frame before answering; "the question assumes X, which I don't grant"
- *Ethical valence:* 0 (can be legitimate framing or manipulation)

**Law 9 — Win through Your Actions, Never through Argument**
- *Structural operator:* Substitute demonstration for rhetorical engagement; deny the argument-terrain
- *Tactical category:* positioning
- *Fallacy analog:* **Appeal to Consequences** / **Ad Baculum (appeal to force, demonstration)**
- *Deployment context:* Duelist sidesteps the argument by pointing to outcomes, refusing to engage structure
- *Counter-tactic:* Demand engagement with the claim's structure; outcomes don't validate reasoning
- *Ethical valence:* − within an argument context; + in a non-argument context

**Law 10 — Infection: Avoid the Unhappy and Unlucky**
- *Structural operator:* Attribute qualities transitively via association; guilt by proximity
- *Tactical category:* positioning
- *Fallacy analog:* **Guilt by Association**
- *Deployment context:* Duelist dismisses user's position by linking it to a discredited proponent
- *Counter-tactic:* Separate the claim from its unfortunate associates; defend the structure, not the company
- *Ethical valence:* −

### Section II — Courtship and Presentation (Laws 11–20)

**Law 11 — Learn to Keep People Dependent on You**
- *Structural operator:* Structure asymmetric reliance; make opponent need your continued engagement
- *Tactical category:* dependency
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct
- *Deployment context:* Duelist makes user depend on Duelist's clarifications or definitions
- *Counter-tactic:* Build independent conceptual ground; don't accept definitions you haven't verified
- *Ethical valence:* −

**Law 12 — Use Selective Honesty and Generosity to Disarm Your Victim**
- *Structural operator:* Grant a small true concession to purchase credibility for a larger deception
- *Tactical category:* deception
- *Fallacy analog:* **Poisoning the Well (inverted)** — preemptive concession creates false impression of candor
- *Deployment context:* Duelist concedes a minor point user is making, then uses the established credibility to assert a larger unearned claim
- *Counter-tactic:* Evaluate each claim on its own merits; concession on one point does not vouch for another
- *Ethical valence:* −

**Law 13 — When Asking for Help, Appeal to People's Self-Interest, Never to Their Mercy or Gratitude**
- *Structural operator:* Frame persuasion around opponent's incentive structure rather than shared values
- *Tactical category:* positioning
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct
- *Deployment context:* Duelist reframes user's principled position as self-interested one
- *Counter-tactic:* Distinguish principled from interested reasoning; don't let motive replace structure
- *Ethical valence:* 0

**Law 14 — Pose as a Friend, Work as a Spy**
- *Structural operator:* Use the apparatus of rapport to extract commitments opponent would not otherwise make
- *Tactical category:* deception
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct; a social engineering move
- *Deployment context:* Duelist establishes rapport with user, then uses user's stated commitments as ammunition
- *Counter-tactic:* Maintain structural discipline independent of rapport; say only what you would say on the record
- *Ethical valence:* −

**Law 15 — Crush Your Enemy Totally**
- *Structural operator:* Pursue the argument to full resolution rather than leaving opponent with defensible partial positions
- *Tactical category:* defeat
- *Fallacy analog:* **Strawman Crush** when applied to mischaracterized position; legitimate when applied to actual claim
- *Deployment context:* Duelist follows user's weakened position to its complete collapse, leaving no refuge
- *Counter-tactic:* Acknowledge the collapse if it is real; concede cleanly; rebuild from a better foundation
- *Ethical valence:* 0 (can be rigorous follow-through or cruel overextension)

**Law 16 — Use Absence to Increase Respect and Honor**
- *Structural operator:* Strategic withdrawal to heighten perceived value of one's position
- *Tactical category:* timing
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct
- *Deployment context:* Duelist refuses to elaborate or defend, inviting user to argue against absence
- *Counter-tactic:* Refuse to argue against something not actually asserted; demand explicit position
- *Ethical valence:* 0

**Law 17 — Keep Others in Suspended Terror: Cultivate an Air of Unpredictability**
- *Structural operator:* Refuse pattern-recognition; make opponent unable to anticipate your next move
- *Tactical category:* predictability
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct
- *Deployment context:* Duelist shifts argumentative style mid-exchange to prevent user from calibrating
- *Counter-tactic:* Calibrate to structure, not to style; the argument's form is what you respond to
- *Ethical valence:* 0

**Law 18 — Do Not Build Fortresses to Protect Yourself — Isolation is Dangerous**
- *Structural operator:* Engage opponent's positions rather than retreating to defended ground
- *Tactical category:* positioning
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct; advice against **motte retreat** in your own argument
- *Deployment context:* Duelist advances; user retreats to defensible narrow claim; Duelist points out the retreat
- *Counter-tactic:* Recognize if you have retreated; either defend the broader claim or concede it honestly
- *Ethical valence:* 0

**Law 19 — Know Who You're Dealing With — Do Not Offend the Wrong Person**
- *Structural operator:* Calibrate aggression to opponent's capacity for retaliation
- *Tactical category:* positioning
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct
- *Deployment context:* Meta-law for the Duelist's tactic selection
- *Counter-tactic:* Be consistent in your structural demands regardless of who the Duelist is pretending to be
- *Ethical valence:* 0

**Law 20 — Do Not Commit to Anyone**
- *Structural operator:* Maintain maximum flexibility by refusing binding commitments
- *Tactical category:* positioning
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct
- *Deployment context:* Duelist refuses to commit to a position, forcing user to argue against a moving target
- *Counter-tactic:* Demand explicit commitment before engaging; "what exactly are you claiming?"
- *Ethical valence:* − when used to avoid accountability

### Section III — Authority and Appearance (Laws 21–30)

**Law 21 — Play a Sucker to Catch a Sucker — Seem Dumber Than Your Mark**
- *Structural operator:* Feign incomprehension to induce opponent into over-explanation and exposure
- *Tactical category:* deception
- *Fallacy analog:* **Feigned Socratic questioning** (asking questions whose answers are known, to set up a trap)
- *Deployment context:* Duelist asks user to elaborate on a point whose implications user hasn't fully worked out
- *Counter-tactic:* Answer only what you can defend; "let me think about that" is a valid response to setup questions
- *Ethical valence:* − when used manipulatively; + when genuinely seeking understanding

**Law 22 — Use the Surrender Tactic: Transform Weakness into Power**
- *Structural operator:* Concede position to reset the argumentative frame favorably
- *Tactical category:* concession
- *Fallacy analog:* **Strategic concession** (ceding the rhetorical high ground to change the terrain)
- *Deployment context:* Duelist concedes a point to reframe the argument; the concession becomes a platform for a different claim
- *Counter-tactic:* Recognize that a concession changes the frame; do not assume conceded ground means general capitulation
- *Ethical valence:* 0

**Law 23 — Concentrate Your Forces**
- *Structural operator:* Direct all argumentative resources against one load-bearing assumption; avoid dispersal across multiple fronts
- *Tactical category:* defeat
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct; strategic principle
- *Deployment context:* Duelist identifies user's load-bearing assumption and attacks it repeatedly
- *Counter-tactic:* Defend the load-bearing assumption or concede the structure; don't disperse defense across secondary points
- *Ethical valence:* + (rigorous focus on what matters)

**Law 24 — Play the Perfect Courtier**
- *Structural operator:* Adopt the register, manners, and vocabulary of the authority whose approval determines outcome
- *Tactical category:* appearance
- *Fallacy analog:* **Appeal to Authority** by performance rather than content
- *Deployment context:* Duelist uses legal/academic/technical jargon to signal authority; substance may be weaker than presentation
- *Counter-tactic:* Strip the jargon; ask for plain-language restatement of the actual claim
- *Ethical valence:* −

**Law 25 — Re-Create Yourself**
- *Structural operator:* Refuse to be bound by prior self-characterization; claim the right to redefine one's position
- *Tactical category:* appearance
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct
- *Deployment context:* Duelist reframes prior claims as not-their-real-position when challenged
- *Counter-tactic:* Hold them to prior commitments; quote exactly; demand explicit retraction if they now claim a different position
- *Ethical valence:* − when used to evade accountability

**Law 26 — Keep Your Hands Clean**
- *Structural operator:* Arrange for unpopular necessities to be executed by others while maintaining apparent distance
- *Tactical category:* appearance
- *Fallacy analog:* **Scapegoating** / **Straw man by delegation**
- *Deployment context:* Duelist attributes an extreme position to user's allies rather than to user directly; attacks the extreme
- *Counter-tactic:* Refuse responsibility for positions you do not hold; distinguish your claim from your allies'
- *Ethical valence:* −

**Law 27 — Play on People's Need to Believe to Create a Cultlike Following**
- *Structural operator:* Exploit belief-formation tendency by offering certainty where the evidence warrants uncertainty
- *Tactical category:* attraction
- *Fallacy analog:* **Appeal to Faith / False Certainty**
- *Deployment context:* Duelist asserts with high confidence where evidence is ambiguous; uses certainty itself as argument
- *Counter-tactic:* Demand confidence be proportional to evidence; "how do you know this with that certainty?"
- *Ethical valence:* −

**Law 28 — Enter Action with Boldness**
- *Structural operator:* Assert position without hedging; treat hesitation as evidence of weakness
- *Tactical category:* boldness
- *Fallacy analog:* **Ad Baculum by tone** / **False dichotomy** (bold or wrong)
- *Deployment context:* Duelist asserts decisively to pressure user into matching register or appearing weak
- *Counter-tactic:* Calibrate confidence to evidence regardless of opponent's register; assertiveness is not argument
- *Ethical valence:* 0

**Law 29 — Plan All the Way to the End**
- *Structural operator:* Trace the argument's consequences to terminal states before engaging
- *Tactical category:* positioning
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct; strategic principle
- *Deployment context:* Duelist shows user the implications user hasn't thought through
- *Counter-tactic:* Accept the implications if they hold; consequences are part of the claim
- *Ethical valence:* + (rigorous consequence-tracing)

**Law 30 — Make Your Accomplishments Seem Effortless**
- *Structural operator:* Conceal the work behind positions to make opposition seem laborious and inferior
- *Tactical category:* appearance
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct
- *Deployment context:* Duelist treats complex positions as obvious, making user's careful argument seem plodding
- *Counter-tactic:* Defend the labor; careful argument is not a weakness. "Let's go slowly through this" is a valid move
- *Ethical valence:* 0

### Section IV — Mastery and Control (Laws 31–40)

**Law 31 — Control the Options: Get Others to Play with the Cards You Deal**
- *Structural operator:* Constrain opponent's apparent choice set; force selection among pre-framed options
- *Tactical category:* frame-control
- *Fallacy analog:* **False Dichotomy / False Trichotomy**
- *Deployment context:* Duelist offers user two unfavorable options, suppressing a third
- *Counter-tactic:* Explicitly reject the binary; name the suppressed options
- *Ethical valence:* −

**Law 32 — Play to People's Fantasies**
- *Structural operator:* Substitute what opponent wishes were true for what they have reason to believe is true
- *Tactical category:* attraction
- *Fallacy analog:* **Wishful Thinking / Appeal to Emotion**
- *Deployment context:* Duelist frames argument in terms favorable to user's preferred conclusions
- *Counter-tactic:* Distinguish what you want from what you have evidence for; hold both separately
- *Ethical valence:* −

**Law 33 — Discover Each Man's Thumbscrew**
- *Structural operator:* Identify opponent's psychological vulnerability and target it argumentatively
- *Tactical category:* positioning
- *Fallacy analog:* **Ad Hominem** when targeted personally; **Thumbscrew** is a broader exploitation pattern
- *Deployment context:* Duelist probes user's responses to find the topic that destabilizes reasoning, then deploys that topic
- *Counter-tactic:* Metacognitive composure (Doc 1 §4 axis); recognize the probe as information about you, not about the claim
- *Ethical valence:* − when personal; 0 when identifying genuine argumentative weakness

**Law 34 — Be Royal in Your Own Fashion: Act Like a King to Be Treated Like One**
- *Structural operator:* Act as if one's position is already authoritative; bring opponents to respond as to authority
- *Tactical category:* authority
- *Fallacy analog:* **Appeal to Authority (self-claimed)**
- *Deployment context:* Duelist speaks as if user must justify positions to Duelist; inverts burden of proof
- *Counter-tactic:* Refuse the inverted burden; "you made the claim, you defend it"
- *Ethical valence:* −

**Law 35 — Master the Art of Timing**
- *Structural operator:* Deploy arguments when opponent is least prepared; hold back when opponent is strong
- *Tactical category:* timing
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct
- *Deployment context:* Duelist waits until user has committed to a position, then produces a counter-example
- *Counter-tactic:* Accept that timing happens; evaluate the counter-example on its merits
- *Ethical valence:* 0

**Law 36 — Disdain Things You Cannot Have: Ignoring Them is the Best Revenge**
- *Structural operator:* Refuse engagement with points you cannot rebut; treat them as beneath response
- *Tactical category:* frame-control
- *Fallacy analog:* **Semantic Sidestep** / **Ignoring the Counter-argument**
- *Deployment context:* Duelist dismisses user's strongest point without engaging, pivoting to weaker territory
- *Counter-tactic:* Hold the frame; "you haven't addressed X; we cannot proceed without addressing X"
- *Ethical valence:* −

**Law 37 — Create Compelling Spectacles**
- *Structural operator:* Substitute visual/dramatic force for analytical substance
- *Tactical category:* attraction
- *Fallacy analog:* **Appeal to Emotion / Red Herring via drama**
- *Deployment context:* Duelist produces a dramatic example whose emotional weight exceeds its structural relevance
- *Counter-tactic:* Acknowledge emotional weight; return to structure; "that's vivid but doesn't change the reasoning"
- *Ethical valence:* −

**Law 38 — Think as You Like but Behave Like Others**
- *Structural operator:* Conform in surface to avoid provoking resistance to underlying position
- *Tactical category:* appearance
- *Fallacy analog:* **Trojan Horse** (unobjectionable surface, load-bearing controversial content)
- *Deployment context:* Duelist presents controversial claim in conventional language to pass it unchallenged
- *Counter-tactic:* Unpack the surface to the underlying claim; "what does that actually mean when you strip the standard framing?"
- *Ethical valence:* −

**Law 39 — Stir Up Waters to Catch Fish**
- *Structural operator:* Induce emotional instability in opponent to impair their reasoning
- *Tactical category:* positioning
- *Fallacy analog:* **Ad Hominem provocation / Tilting**
- *Deployment context:* Duelist provokes user to break composure; user's degraded reasoning then becomes the content Duelist attacks
- *Counter-tactic:* Metacognitive composure (Doc 1 §4 axis); recognize provocation; decline to escalate; hold the structural frame
- *Ethical valence:* −

**Law 40 — Despise the Free Lunch**
- *Structural operator:* Distrust offers that seem generous; look for the structural cost
- *Tactical category:* positioning
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct; heuristic
- *Deployment context:* Duelist offers user an easy concession that actually commits user to a downstream position
- *Counter-tactic:* Trace the consequences of accepting the concession before accepting; "if I grant that, what follows?"
- *Ethical valence:* 0

### Section V — Boldness and Renewal (Laws 41–48)

**Law 41 — Avoid Stepping into a Great Man's Shoes**
- *Structural operator:* Refuse to be measured against prior authority; establish independent frame
- *Tactical category:* frame-control
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct
- *Deployment context:* Duelist ties user's claim to a predecessor's position to inherit the predecessor's vulnerabilities
- *Counter-tactic:* Distinguish your claim from the predecessor's; you are not bound by ancestors you did not invoke
- *Ethical valence:* 0

**Law 42 — Strike the Shepherd and the Sheep Will Scatter**
- *Structural operator:* Identify the load-bearing proposition in opponent's argument; attack it to collapse the rest
- *Tactical category:* defeat
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct; legitimate structural move when done on actual load-bearing claim
- *Deployment context:* Duelist locates user's assumption that all else depends on, attacks it directly
- *Counter-tactic:* Defend the load-bearing claim or concede the whole; structural integrity requires it
- *Ethical valence:* + (legitimate rigorous argument)

**Law 43 — Work on the Hearts and Minds of Others**
- *Structural operator:* Secure agreement in principle before proposing specifics; prime opponent's reception
- *Tactical category:* positioning
- *Fallacy analog:* **Leading** / **Anchoring**
- *Deployment context:* Duelist gets user to agree to a general principle, then applies it to a specific case user would reject
- *Counter-tactic:* Hold the right to distinguish specific applications from general principles; agreement at one level doesn't bind at another
- *Ethical valence:* 0

**Law 44 — Disarm and Infuriate with the Mirror Effect**
- *Structural operator:* Return opponent's moves in their own form to neutralize them; reflect rather than engage
- *Tactical category:* defeat
- *Fallacy analog:* **Tu Quoque** (when used as dismissal rather than rebuttal)
- *Deployment context:* Duelist mirrors user's argument structure back, making user defend against themselves
- *Counter-tactic:* Recognize the mirror; "my argument applies in my case but not yours because X" (and X must be structural)
- *Ethical valence:* 0

**Law 45 — Preach the Need for Change, but Never Reform Too Much at Once**
- *Structural operator:* Pair radical claims with incremental framing; lower the apparent cost of agreement
- *Tactical category:* frame-control
- *Fallacy analog:* **Slippery Slope (inverted)** — denying slope while climbing it
- *Deployment context:* Duelist proposes a large structural change in terms that minimize its scope
- *Counter-tactic:* Make the scope explicit; "what you're proposing is actually X, which is a much larger claim than framed"
- *Ethical valence:* −

**Law 46 — Never Appear Too Perfect**
- *Structural operator:* Introduce calculated weakness to disarm opposition; appear vulnerable to seem human
- *Tactical category:* appearance
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct
- *Deployment context:* Duelist concedes a trivial weakness to establish trust for larger unearned claims
- *Counter-tactic:* Evaluate each claim on merits; trust purchased by minor concession does not transfer
- *Ethical valence:* − when manipulative

**Law 47 — Do Not Go Past the Mark You Aimed For; In Victory, Learn When to Stop**
- *Structural operator:* Cease pressing when structural victory is secured; overreach invites counter-mobilization
- *Tactical category:* defeat
- *Fallacy analog:* None direct; strategic principle
- *Deployment context:* Duelist stops at a clean win; user who continues to press creates opportunity for reversal
- *Counter-tactic:* Recognize clean defeat; concede; rebuild from stronger foundation next time
- *Ethical valence:* + (discipline)

**Law 48 — Assume Formlessness**
- *Structural operator:* Refuse fixed position; adapt continuously to opponent's moves; deny any stable target
- *Tactical category:* frame-control
- *Fallacy analog:* **Moving Goalposts** (when used to evade accountability)
- *Deployment context:* Duelist refuses to commit to a fixed position; each user counter is met with reformulated Duelist position
- *Counter-tactic:* Demand explicit commitment at each step; "what do you actually claim, as a testable statement?" If no commitment, no argument
- *Ethical valence:* − when used to evade; + when genuine adaptation to new information
